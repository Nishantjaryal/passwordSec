How to identify strength of password?
Identifying the strength of a password typically involves evaluating several criteria. A common method is to use a scoring matrix or algorithm that assesses various attributes of the password. Here are some factors commonly considered:

1. Length:
- Longer passwords are generally stronger. A typical recommendation is at least 12-16 characters.

2. Character Variety:
- Use of uppercase letters (A-Z)
- Use of lowercase letters (a-z)
- Use of digits (0-9)
- Use of special characters (e.g., !, @, #, $, etc.)

3. Avoidance of Common Patterns:
- Avoiding easily guessable sequences like "123456", "password", "abcdef", etc.
- Avoiding common substitutions (e.g., "P@ssw0rd" instead of "Password")

4. Entropy:
- Measure of unpredictability. Higher entropy indicates a stronger password.

5. Dictionary Words:
- Avoiding entire dictionary words or common phrases.

6. Repetition and Predictable Patterns:
- Avoiding repeated characters or predictable patterns like "aaa111" or "abcd1234".

A basic scoring matrix could look like this:

Criteria Points (Example)
Length: 8-11 characters 1 point
Length: 12-15 characters 2 points
Length: 16+ characters 3 points
Contains lowercase 1 point
Contains uppercase 1 point
Contains digits 1 point
Contains special characters 1 point
No common words/patterns 1 point
High entropy 2 points

A password could be classified based on the total points:

- Weak: 0-3 points
- Moderate: 4-6 points
- Strong: 7-9 points
- Very Strong: 10+ points

For more sophisticated assessments, tools like zxcvbn (an open-source password strength estimator) can be used. These tools analyze password strength more comprehensively by considering a larger variety of factors and utilizing advanced algorithms.
